What is Obesity, and Is It Dangerous?

Obesity is defined as excessive body fat accumulation that poses health risks, characterized by a Body Mass Index (BMI) of 30 or higher. Obesity significantly increases the risk of serious conditions including diabetes, high blood pressure, abnormal cholesterol levels, heart disease, stroke, certain types of cancer, and osteoarthritis. Ultimately, this research is beneficial for increasing overall awareness of a problem that is becoming more common over time in NYC.
